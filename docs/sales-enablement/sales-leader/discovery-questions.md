# Account Insights — Discovery Questions (Sales Leaders)

**Purpose:** Uncover account management gaps and coverage pain that map directly to Account Insights. These questions are designed for VPs of Sales, CROs, and Sales Managers.

**Principle:** Sales leaders think in accounts, territories, and team performance — not data architecture. Every question should trigger a specific account or situation they remember.

---

## Account Prioritization & Coverage

These questions surface the ICP Fit and Account Neglect use cases.

**1. "Think about your team's account book. Do you know which accounts your reps spent the most time on last quarter — and were they the right ones?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "Honestly, I'm not sure"
- "Some reps are great at prioritization, others chase whatever's in front of them"
- "We give them target account lists but I don't know if they're working them"

*Bridge to ICP Fit:*
> "What if you could see which accounts in your reps' books actually match your best-customer behavioral profile — not just industry and size, but the engagement patterns that match how your closed-won deals behaved? Your top 20 prospect accounts ranked by behavioral fit, updated in real time. That changes how your team plans their week."

---

**2. "When was the last time you discovered a high-value customer account that your team hadn't touched in weeks?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "It happens more than I'd like"
- A specific account or story (this is gold — dig in)
- "We rely on CSMs to flag that, but it's not always timely"

*Bridge to Account Neglect:*
> "What if you had a view that showed you every account above $500K in ARR where your team has had zero meetings in 30 days? Not a monthly report — a live view you check before every account review. The accounts that are quietly slipping away because everyone's focused on something else."

---

**3. "How do your reps decide which accounts to prioritize on a given day? Out of 100+ accounts, how do they pick where to spend their time?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "They follow their pipeline — accounts with active deals"
- "We have target lists but reps have their own system"
- "The best reps have good instincts, the average ones spray and pray"

*Bridge to Account Insights:*
> "Most reps default to accounts with active deals or whoever called them last. But the account that deserves their time most might be the prospect that matches your ICP perfectly and just had two execs engage for the first time — or the $1M customer where engagement has been declining for 3 weeks. Account Insights gives every rep the prioritization instincts of your best rep — backed by behavioral data."

## Relationship Visibility

These questions surface the Relationship Health use case.

**4. "How many of your key accounts are single-threaded — where your entire relationship depends on one person?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "More than I'd like to admit"
- "I tell reps to multi-thread but I can't always see if they're doing it"
- "We had a champion leave last year and almost lost the account"

*Bridge to Relationship Health:*
> "People.ai already tracks who's in the meetings and on the emails. Account Insights turns that into a relationship map: this account has 12 contacts engaged across 3 departments, this account has 1 contact and it's the same person for 6 months. You can see which accounts need relationship broadening before the single thread breaks."

---

**5. "When you look across your customer base, do you have visibility into which accounts have executive-level relationships and which don't?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "Not really — I'd have to ask each rep"
- "We track it in CRM but it's inconsistent"
- "Exec engagement is something we talk about but don't measure"

*Bridge to Relationship Health:*
> "Account Insights shows exec coverage across every account — which accounts have VPs and above in meetings, which ones are stuck at the individual contributor level. For a $2M account, knowing that zero executives have been in a meeting in the last 60 days is a coaching conversation you want to have now, not during the renewal."

---

## Expansion & Growth

These questions surface the Whitespace & Expansion and Engagement Momentum use cases.

**6. "How does your team identify expansion opportunities in existing accounts? Is it systematic or does it depend on the rep?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "It's mostly rep-driven"
- "CSMs flag it sometimes"
- "We don't have a great process for this"
- "We look at usage data" (good sign — they value signals)

*Bridge to Whitespace & Expansion:*
> "Account Insights can show you every customer account where engagement is 3x above what you'd expect for their current spend. They're attending events, new departments are reaching out, meeting frequency is surging — but there's no expansion pipeline. These accounts are telling you they want more. Would you want to see which of your customers look like that right now?"

---

**7. "When an account starts heating up — new execs engaging, meeting frequency increasing, inbound emails spiking — how quickly does your team notice?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "If the rep is paying attention, pretty quickly"
- "We usually don't see it until there's an active deal"
- "Honestly, we probably miss it"

*Bridge to Engagement Momentum:*
> "Those are your momentum signals. Account Insights compares 7-day activity against the 30-day baseline for every account. When engagement surges — new exec meetings, email volume tripling, in-person visits — it surfaces immediately. The difference between catching a buying signal in week 1 versus week 4 is often the difference between winning and losing."

---

**8. "What's the biggest customer account your team lost or nearly lost in the last year that you think you should have saved?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- A specific account (this is gold — ask follow-up questions)
- What went wrong
- When they realized it was in trouble

*Bridge to Account Neglect + Relationship Health:*
> "Walk me through what the engagement looked like in the 90 days before you realized it was at risk. Were meetings still happening? Was the champion still responsive? Were emails going both ways? In most cases, the behavioral data showed the account was in trouble months before anyone noticed — engagement declining, meeting frequency dropping, exec access disappearing. That's exactly what Account Insights catches."

## Team Performance

**9. "If you could give every rep on your team one superpower for managing their book, what would it be?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "Knowing which accounts to focus on"
- "Seeing which customers are at risk"
- "Identifying expansion opportunities"
- "Better account prioritization"

*Bridge to Account Insights:*
> "That's the core idea. Instead of reps deciding by gut feel which accounts to work today, they open their book and see: these 5 accounts match your ICP and are showing buying signals, this customer hasn't heard from you in 30 days, this account's engagement just surged — something's happening. Every rep gets the account instincts of your best rep."

---

**10. "How do your best reps manage their territory differently from your average reps?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Best reps are strategic about account selection
- Average reps react to inbound or work deals sequentially
- Best reps spot expansion and risk earlier
- The gap is usually prioritization and pattern recognition

*Bridge to Account Insights:*
> "The best reps have account intuition — they just *know* which accounts to focus on and which ones need attention. But that doesn't scale. Account Insights gives every rep the same behavioral intelligence your best rep has — backed by engagement data instead of gut feel. It levels up the whole team."

---

## Sequencing Advice

**Opening the conversation:** Start with question 1 (account prioritization) or 8 (the account they almost lost). These are emotional — sales leaders remember specific accounts.

**Going deeper:** If they engage on prioritization, go to questions 2-3. If they engage on relationship risk, go to questions 4-5. If they engage on expansion, go to questions 6-7.

**Closing toward a trial:** End with question 9 or 10 (rep superpowers). It naturally leads to "what if I could set this up for your next account review?"

**Key difference from RevOps discovery:** Sales leaders think in *specific accounts* and *specific reps*. When they share an example, dig into it. "Tell me more about that account" is more powerful than moving to the next question.
