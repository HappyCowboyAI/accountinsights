# Account Insights — Narrative Talk Track (Sales Leaders)

**Audience:** VPs of Sales, CROs, Sales Managers
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes (expandable to 30 with demo)
**Tone:** Outcome-driven, account-focused, revenue-oriented

---

## The Story Arc

```
OPEN: Your reps are spending 60% of their time on the wrong accounts
  -> PROBLEM: You can't see what's happening across your entire book
    -> IMPACT: What it costs your number
      -> SOLUTION: Account intelligence that tells your team where to focus
        -> PROOF: Three accounts your team would have missed
          -> CLOSE: See it on your account portfolio this week
```

---

## 1. OPEN — The Accounts You're Not Watching (2 min)

> "How many accounts in your team's book right now have a problem that nobody's told you about?"

Pause.

> "Here's the reality. Your reps have 50, 100, maybe 200 accounts assigned to them. They know their top 5 — the ones with active deals, the ones they're excited about. But the other 95% of the book? Running on autopilot. And somewhere in there, a $2M customer hasn't heard from anyone on your team in 30 days. A prospect account is surging with engagement — new execs joining meetings, email volume tripling — and nobody's noticed. An expansion opportunity is practically waving a flag and no rep has created a deal.

> You find out when the customer churns. Or when a competitor shows up. Or at the end of the quarter, when you realize your team has been pouring time into accounts that were never going to close — while high-potential accounts sat untouched.

> The data knew. Nobody was reading it."

**Why this works for sales leaders:** You're naming their lived experience. Every VP of Sales has discovered too late that a key account went dark or that reps were chasing the wrong targets. You're telling them there's a way to prevent it.

---

## 2. PROBLEM — Your Team Can't See What the Data Sees (3 min)

> "Your reps are good. But they're human. And humans are bad at evaluating 100 accounts simultaneously against behavioral patterns. Here are four things that happen in every sales org that your team almost never catches in time."

**High-value accounts going neglected.**

> "A customer account worth $2M in ARR had regular meetings through Q1 — weekly check-ins, QBRs on schedule, champion engaged. Then Q2 starts and the rep gets pulled into new business. The meetings stop. The emails dry up. But the account is still sitting in your CRM, green status, because the rep hasn't flagged anything. Meanwhile, the customer is talking to your competitor. By the time someone notices, the renewal conversation is already poisoned."

**Reps working the wrong accounts.**

> "Your team has 200 prospect accounts. Twenty of them look like your best customers — same engagement patterns, same exec involvement, same meeting cadence as the accounts that closed last year. But your reps don't know which 20. So they spread their time across all 200 equally, or worse, they follow their gut. Meanwhile, the accounts that actually match your winning profile aren't getting the attention they deserve."

**Expansion hiding in plain sight.**

> "You have customer accounts where engagement is 3x above average — they're attending every webinar, their execs are requesting meetings, email volume is surging. These accounts are telling you they want more. But nobody on your team has noticed because no one is comparing engagement levels across the customer base. There's no open pipeline, no expansion plan — just invisible demand."

**Single-threaded relationships masking risk.**

> "Your rep says the account is solid. They have a great relationship with the champion. But it's one person. One thread. If that champion changes roles, gets promoted, or leaves — the entire account is at risk. Your team doesn't see it because the engagement score looks fine. It's the breadth of the relationship that tells the real story."

**Why this works for sales leaders:** These aren't abstract data problems — they're management failures they recognize. You're describing situations where they should have stepped in but didn't have visibility.

---

## 3. IMPACT — What It Costs Your Number (2 min)

> "Let me translate this into what it means for your revenue."

Tailor to their team size if you know it:

> "For a team of 50 reps:

> - **15-20% of prospecting time wasted on wrong accounts.** Your reps are spending hours on accounts that don't match your winning profile. ICP-based behavioral scoring reclaims that time and points it at accounts that actually look like your best customers. That's a 15-20% improvement in prospecting efficiency.

> - **$1M to $2M in relationships at risk.** Accounts where the engagement is single-threaded or the relationship depth doesn't match the account value. One champion departure and the account is in trouble. Early detection means early multi-threading — before the risk becomes a renewal crisis.

> - **$750K to $1.5M in expansion left on the table.** Customer accounts with 3x the engagement of similar-sized accounts — they're practically asking for more. But nobody's created a deal because no one's comparing engagement patterns across the base.

> - **$500K to $1M in pipeline from neglected accounts.** High-value accounts your team hasn't touched in 30 days. These aren't lost causes — they're invisible neglect. A single outreach to a neglected $500K account could reactivate pipeline that doesn't exist today.

> Add it up: **$3M to $6M+ in influenced revenue.** Not from hiring more reps. Not from a new tool. From pointing your team at the right accounts with the right intelligence."

**Why this works for sales leaders:** Revenue is their language. "Influenced revenue" maps directly to their quota. And "pointing your team" positions this as a management lever, not a monitoring tool.

## 4. SOLUTION — Account Intelligence That Tells Your Team Where to Focus (5 min)

> "Account Insights doesn't replace your account reviews or your team's judgment. It makes both dramatically better by showing you which accounts deserve attention — and why.

> Think of it this way: right now, a rep decides which accounts to work based on gut feel, recent activity, or whoever happens to call. With Account Insights, they open their day and see: 'These 5 accounts match your best-customer profile and are showing buying signals. These 3 customers haven't heard from you in 30 days. This account has 3x the engagement of similar-sized customers — time to explore expansion.' That's a completely different workday."

### The Five Things It Watches For

> "It monitors every account in your book — not just the ones with active deals — for five patterns:

> 1. **ICP Fit** — which prospect accounts behave like your best customers. Not just firmographics like industry and size, but behavioral signals: engagement patterns, exec involvement, meeting cadence. The accounts that match your closed-won profile.

> 2. **Relationship Health** — how deep and broad your relationships are across every account. Multi-threaded or single-threaded? Exec coverage or just one champion? Responsive emails or one-directional? The real health of the account, not the rep's self-assessment.

> 3. **Engagement Momentum** — which accounts are heating up and which are cooling down, right now. A surge in meetings, new execs engaging, inbound email spikes — these are buying signals. Declining activity on a key account is an early warning.

> 4. **Whitespace and Expansion** — customer accounts where engagement far exceeds what you'd expect for their current footprint. These accounts are telling you they want more. Time to create pipeline.

> 5. **Account Neglect** — high-value accounts your team hasn't touched. No meetings in 30 days, declining engagement, zero outbound email. The accounts that are quietly slipping away because everyone's focused on something else."

### Where Your Team Sees It

> "Three places — and this is important because it means your reps don't have to go looking for it.

> **On the account itself** — SalesAI Signals appear right on the account record. When a rep opens an account, the most critical insight is already there with a recommended action.

> **In Slack** — any rep types `/ai` and gets a full account intelligence digest in under 3 minutes. Neglected accounts, surging accounts, expansion candidates — all in one message.

> **In your account reviews** — pre-built views that show you exactly which accounts need attention and why. Sort by ICP fit, sort by neglect severity, sort by expansion potential. Instead of reviewing accounts alphabetically, you're reviewing by intelligence."

**Why this works for sales leaders:** "Account intelligence" reframes this as a strategic management tool. The workday example is visceral — every manager knows the difference between reps who work smart and reps who spray and pray.

## 5. PROOF — Three Accounts Your Team Would Have Missed (5 min)

### Path A: Live Demo

> "Let me show you your account book through this lens."

Open the Account Neglect table. Sort by annual revenue descending.

> "These are your highest-value accounts that your team has stopped engaging. [Point to the biggest account.] This one — $XXM in ARR, zero meetings in the last 30 days, engagement score of XX. If this were your rep's key account, what would you want to ask them?"

Let them react. Then:

> "Now look at this." Open the Whitespace & Expansion table.

> "These are customer accounts where engagement is way above average for their current spend. They're attending events, their execs are requesting meetings, email volume is surging. But there's no expansion pipeline. How much of this is demand your team isn't capturing?"

### Path B: Scenario Walkthrough

> "Let me give you three specific situations.

> **The $2M customer nobody's talked to.** One of your top-10 customers by ARR hasn't had a meeting with anyone on your team in 30 days. The engagement score has dropped from 75 to 25. The rep says they're 'giving the customer space after the renewal.' But a competitor just hired an AE who used to work at that customer's company. With Account Insights, you see this the moment the engagement drops — not when the renewal is at risk. You coach the rep to schedule a QBR, re-engage the exec sponsor, and lock down the relationship.

> **The prospect account that matches your winners.** Your team closed a $500K deal last quarter at a manufacturing company with 2,000 employees. The deal had a pattern: 12 meetings in the first 60 days, 3 exec meetings, bi-directional email flow, engagement score above 70. Account Insights finds 8 other prospect accounts with the exact same behavioral profile — same engagement pattern, same exec involvement, same cadence. But your reps have these ranked the same as every other account in their territory. You reprioritize the team's focus and 3 of those 8 accounts enter pipeline within 30 days.

> **The expansion opportunity waving a flag.** A mid-market customer paying $200K has engagement that looks like your $500K customers — 3x the meeting volume, new departments reaching out, VPs joining calls who were never involved before. This account is telling you they want more. But there's no expansion deal in CRM because nobody compared their engagement to the benchmark. You flag it to the account owner, they run a whitespace analysis, and the account expands to $450K."

## 6. CLOSE — See It On Your Account Book This Week (2 min)

> "Here's what I'd suggest. Let me set this up on your account data this week. It takes minutes — literally. No IT ticket, no schema changes, no rep training required for the first step.

> I'll deploy the five insight views on your People.ai instance. You go into your next account review with the Neglect table and ICP Fit table open alongside your normal view. Just see what it surfaces. If every neglected account it flags is one you already knew about — no harm done. But I'll bet you at least 2-3 accounts show up that nobody was watching.

> That's the pilot. One account review, your real data, see what you learn. From there we can add the SalesAI Signals so reps see insights on every account, and the Slack digest so your team can self-serve.

> What's your next account or territory review? Let's have this ready for it."

**Why this works for sales leaders:** You're not asking for a 30-day evaluation or a procurement process. You're asking to be ready for their next account review — that could be this Thursday. The urgency is natural and the commitment is minimal.
