# Account Insights — Discovery Questions

**Purpose:** Uncover pain that maps directly to Account Insights capabilities. These questions are designed for RevOps conversations but work across personas.

**Principle:** Never pitch first. Every question should surface a problem they already have. When they describe it, *then* you connect it to the capability.

---

## Account Coverage & Hygiene

These questions surface the Account Neglect and Relationship Health use cases.

**1. "Walk me through how you track account coverage today. How do you know if a high-value account is being properly worked by your team?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Rely on reps to self-report account status
- Quarterly manual audits of CRM activity
- No systematic way to flag coverage gaps between reviews

*Bridge to Account Insights:*
> "What if accounts with zero engagement, declining activity, or single-threaded relationships surfaced automatically — based on activity signals, not rep self-reporting?"

---

**2. "How do you know if a rep is single-threaded on a strategic account? Do you track multi-threading at the account level?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "We look at contact roles in CRM" (lagging indicator)
- "We don't have a good way to measure it across accounts"
- "It comes up in QBRs but we don't track it systematically"

*Bridge to Relationship Health:*
> "People.ai already captures who's in the meetings and on the emails. The question is whether anyone is looking at the pattern — an account with 15 meetings but only 2 contacts engaged is a very different health profile than one with 8 contacts across 3 departments."

---

**3. "When a strategic account goes dark — stops taking meetings, emails go unanswered — how quickly does your team typically notice?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "Usually when renewal comes up"
- "The rep notices but doesn't always escalate"
- "Weeks, sometimes months"

*Bridge to Account Neglect:*
> "What if you could catch that 2-3 weeks earlier — when engagement first starts declining, not after the account has gone completely silent?"

## Scoring & Data Transparency

These questions surface the scoring transparency and FormulaMetric use cases.

**4. "How do you currently assess account health? Do you have a scoring model, and how much do people trust it?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- No scoring model, or one based on firmographic data only
- Black-box vendor scores that reps don't trust
- Manual scoring that's inconsistent and labor-intensive

*Bridge to Transparent Scoring:*
> "One challenge we see is scoring models that nobody trusts because nobody can see how they work. Account Insights builds every score from OOTB activity metrics — meetings held, emails exchanged, exec access, stakeholder breadth. Twelve FormulaMetrics with fully transparent calculations. Your team can see exactly why an account scored the way it did."

---

**5. "When your leadership asks 'which accounts are at risk?' — how quickly can you answer that with data, not opinions?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "It takes days to pull that together"
- "We rely on rep sentiment"
- "Our reports show activity but not risk"

*Bridge to Composite Scoring:*
> "Account Insights scores every account across five categories — ICP fit, relationship health, engagement momentum, expansion potential, and neglect risk. The composite view gives you an instant answer to 'which accounts need attention' — backed by specific metrics, not opinions."

---

**6. "Do you track executive engagement at the account level? How do you know if the right level of stakeholders are involved?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "We track it on key deals but not across accounts"
- "We don't have a good exec coverage metric"
- "Only anecdotally in deal reviews"

*Bridge to Exec Coverage Metrics:*
> "People.ai already captures who's in the meetings. Account Insights computes an exec coverage ratio — what percentage of recent meetings include VP+ stakeholders. An account with 20 meetings and zero exec involvement is a fundamentally different risk profile than one where 40% of meetings include an executive."

## Operational Efficiency & Scale

These questions surface the "why now" and "why automated" angles.

**7. "How many accounts does your team manage across all segments? How many of those get meaningful coverage review in a given quarter?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Hundreds or thousands of accounts, maybe 30-50 get reviewed
- Managers focus on renewal dates and pipeline, not coverage quality
- Mid-market and long-tail accounts get almost no scrutiny

*Bridge to Account Insights:*
> "That's the math problem every RevOps team has. You can't manually audit 500 accounts every quarter. But you can have every account automatically scored across five health dimensions — and only surface the ones that need attention."

---

**8. "What does your current CRM administration look like for account health tracking? How many custom fields or reports have you built?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Heavy custom field maintenance
- Reports that take hours to build and nobody uses
- "We've tried to build this but it's too manual"

*Bridge to Zero-Admin Deployment:*
> "Account Insights requires zero custom fields, zero Salesforce admin work, and zero data migration. It deploys via bookmarklets using existing OOTB metrics. The 12 FormulaMetrics compute everything from data People.ai already captures. No schema changes needed."

---

**9. "How do you handle account coverage reporting today? Is it automated or manual?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Painful quarterly audits
- Manual spreadsheet analysis
- Inconsistent methodology across teams

*Bridge to Automated Coverage Reporting:*
> "What if that reporting happened continuously instead of quarterly? Neglected accounts flagged in real time, coverage gaps surfaced automatically, and relationship health scored against actual engagement patterns — not just what's in the CRM field."

## Expansion & Growth

These questions surface the Whitespace & Expansion and ICP Fit use cases.

**10. "When an account is ripe for expansion — engagement spiking, new departments involved, executive attention increasing — does your team have a systematic way to identify that?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- "Not really — we rely on reps to spot it"
- "We don't always know which accounts are primed for growth"
- "Our expansion plays are reactive, not proactive"

*Bridge to Whitespace & Expansion:*
> "That's revenue left on the table. Accounts with disproportionate engagement relative to current spend — new stakeholders from different departments, exec meetings spiking, activity 3-4x the median — are your expansion candidates. But only if someone notices the pattern and acts on it."

---

## Sequencing Advice

**Opening the conversation:** Start with questions 1, 7, or 8. They're broad and non-threatening.

**Going deeper:** Once they've described a problem, use questions 2-3 (coverage gaps) or 4-5 (scoring and risk) depending on where the energy is.

**Closing toward a pilot:** End with question 9 (coverage reporting) — it naturally leads to "what if this happened automatically?" which sets up the 30-day pilot ask.

**Rule of thumb:** Ask 3-4 questions max. You're having a conversation, not running an interrogation. When they light up on a topic, go deeper there instead of moving to the next question.
